The Best Movies According to Jonnel

My top 3 movies of all-time

The Godfather (1972)

This ultimate crime drama, directed by Francis Ford Coppola,
delves into the complex world of the Corleone family. With
themes of power, loyalty, and betrayal, Marlon Brando’s
portrayal of Vito Corleone has left a lasting legacy in cinema.

Citizen Kane (1941)

Often hailed as the greatest film of all time, Orson Welles' Citizen
Kane explores the rise and fall of media mogul Charles Foster Kane.
Its innovative narrative structure and stunning cinematography
continue to influence filmmakers today.

Pulp Fiction (1994)

Quentin Tarantino’s Pulp Fiction revolutionized storytelling
with its non-linear narrative and eclectic characters. The film’s
arp dialogue and memorable scenes have cemented its status as a cult
classic, showcasing Tarantino's unique style.
